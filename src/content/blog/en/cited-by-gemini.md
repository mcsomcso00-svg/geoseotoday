# How to Get Cited by Gemini and Google AI Overview

**Getting cited by Gemini starts with being trusted in Google's index — because Gemini-powered Google AI Overview draws its sources overwhelmingly from Google's existing search index and ranking signals.** In other words, earning a Gemini citation isn't a separate game: it's the sum of "do your SEO well" plus "write passages in a form that can be quoted." That's what fundamentally separates it from ChatGPT and Perplexity, which run their own crawlers.

## Gemini's citation logic: built on top of Google's index

Gemini is Google's generative model and the engine behind AI Overview. The sources it draws answers from come primarily from **web pages Google has already indexed**, which Gemini then synthesizes, rewrites, and attributes. That carries two practical implications:

1. **Classic SEO signals directly influence Gemini citations.** If a page is poorly indexed by Google and ranks badly, its odds of being chosen as a Gemini source are naturally low.
2. **The bar for citation is "relevant + extractable."** You don't have to rank first, but your passage has to be relevant enough, clean enough, and specific enough for that query for Gemini to pick you. As of 2026, this "relevant and easy to extract" bar decides who gets chosen more than raw domain authority does.

So step one is always: confirm the page is indexed normally by Google. If it isn't in the index, none of the rest matters.

## Six ways to get cited by Gemini

1. **Get indexed by Google first.** Check Search Console; important pages must already be indexed and hold some ranking. No index, no conversation.
2. **Answer-first paragraph.** Open with a self-contained conclusion in 40–80 words — this is the block Gemini finds easiest to quote directly.
3. **Supply citable hard facts.** Concrete numbers, dates, comparison tables. When Gemini synthesizes an answer, it favors passages that give it "facts it can attribute to a source."
4. **Strengthen entities and schema.** Structured data like Article and FAQPage tells Google clearly "this content comes from which trusted brand, written by whom."
5. **Make each H2 self-contained.** The paragraph under every subheading should stand on its own — Gemini often grabs just one section and won't read the whole article.
6. **Keep content fresh.** Stamp dates and update regularly. On time-sensitive queries, Gemini tends to cite more recent sources.

## Gemini citations vs ChatGPT citations: the key difference

The two pull from different retrieval sources, so the optimization emphasis differs slightly:

| Aspect | Gemini / Google AI | ChatGPT |
|---|---|---|
| Source foundation | Google's existing index | OpenAI's own crawlers (GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot) |
| Most critical prerequisite | Get indexed well by Google first | Let GPTBot reach you first |
| Impact of ranking signals | High | More indirect |
| Optimization emphasis | SEO + extractable passages | Crawler access + extractable passages |

The point: **for Gemini, take care of Google SEO; for ChatGPT, take care of crawler access.** What they share is that your passages have to be extractable. For the ChatGPT-specific playbook, see [How to Get ChatGPT to Cite Your Website](/en/blog/cited-by-chatgpt/) in our GEO cluster.

## Watch-outs: three things not to do

- **Don't block Google-Extended.** If you want to be used by Gemini / AI Overview, your robots.txt shouldn't Disallow Google-Extended across the board (for crawler settings, see [AI Crawler List and Management](/en/blog/ai-crawler-management/)).
- **Don't stuff keywords.** Gemini synthesizes meaning, not keyword density; stuffing actually lowers readability and extractability.
- **Don't write only for machines.** One-sided marketing copy and content thin on real information is something generative engines actively avoid.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is getting cited by Gemini the same as appearing in AI Overview?**
They overlap heavily. AI Overview is powered by Gemini, so being chosen as an AI Overview source is, in practice, "being cited by Gemini." The optimization is identical: do your SEO well and keep passages extractable.

**Q2: Do I need to build a dedicated set of content just for Gemini?**
No. The same content standard — answer-first + hard data + schema + self-contained passages — serves Gemini, ChatGPT, and Perplexity at once. The only difference is the prerequisite (SEO vs crawler access), not the content itself.

**Q3: If I'm not in the top ten, can I still be cited by Gemini?**
Yes. Gemini doesn't pick sources strictly by ranking. As long as your passage is relevant, specific, and easy to extract for that query, a mid-ranking page can still be selected — exactly where content clarity beats raw domain authority.

**Q4: How do I confirm whether I'm being cited by Gemini?**
Search your core question on Google to trigger an AI Overview and check whether you're in the source list; or use a visibility tool to track it in bulk. For a systematic approach, see [How to Track AI Search Visibility](/en/blog/track-ai-visibility/).

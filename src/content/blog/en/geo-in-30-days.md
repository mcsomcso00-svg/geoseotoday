# GEO in 30 Days: How Beginners Ship Their First AI-Ready Page

**Here's the 30-day route for a beginner's first GEO-ready page: in week 1, check crawler access and lock in one specific question; in week 2, write ~1,500 words on an "answer-first + self-contained H2" template; in week 3, add data, schema, and an FAQ, then score above 75; in week 4, publish, submit for indexing, and set up a tracking sheet.** One to two focused hours a day is enough.

## Before you start: what does "GEO-ready" mean?

A GEO-ready page clears three bars at once: AI crawlers (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended) can reach it, the structure lets engines extract from it, and the information is specific enough to be worth citing. GeoSeoToday quantifies this with nine signals, and 75 is the publish threshold. The beginner's classic mistake is chasing advanced tactics first; in reality the goal of a first page is simple — **get the fundamentals right once, then copy the process on every article after.**

## Week 1 (Day 1–7): foundations and topic

- **Day 1–2: crawler check.** Open your site's `/robots.txt` and confirm it isn't blocking GPTBot, PerplexityBot, or Google-Extended; check whether your CDN (e.g. Cloudflare) has "block AI crawlers" switched on. No site yet? Spend these two days standing one up (any platform that publishes articles works; your own domain is better).
- **Day 3–4: pick the topic.** Choose one specific question you can answer better than most pages out there. Test it against three criteria: narrow enough ("how to do GEO for e-commerce product pages" beats "GEO tutorial"), you have first-hand experience, and when you ask Perplexity the existing answers fall short.
- **Day 5–7: research.** Put the question to ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google once each, and note: who the current answers cite, what's missing, and which sub-questions People Also Ask surfaces. That list is the raw material for your outline.

## Week 2 (Day 8–14): writing

Write to a fixed skeleton — don't freestyle:

1. **Day 8: write the answer-first paragraph.** 40–80 words, bold, answering the title question in a fully self-contained way. This paragraph matters most and is worth a whole day of polishing.
2. **Day 9–12: write 3–6 H2 body sections.** Each H2 maps to one sub-question from your research; each section stands on its own; use lists for processes and tables for comparisons. Aim for 1,200–2,000 words total.
3. **Day 13: write the FAQ.** Pull 3–5 questions from People Also Ask, each answered in 50–120 words.
4. **Day 14: cool down.** Set it aside for a day and don't look.

For the writing template in detail, see [The GEO Content Structure Template](/en/blog/geo-content-structure/) and [How to Write an Answer-First Opening Paragraph](/en/blog/answer-first-paragraph/).

## Week 3 (Day 15–21): structure and scoring

- **Day 15–16: add hard information.** Reread the whole piece and swap adjectives for numbers, dates, and ranges; confirm at least five concrete data points across the article. Express uncertain numbers as a range — never fabricate.
- **Day 17–18: add schema.** At minimum Article + FAQPage, in JSON-LD. If your platform has a plugin, use it.
- **Day 19: self-containment check.** Paste each H2 section on its own to a friend (or a fresh AI conversation) and ask "does this make sense?" For any section that doesn't, add the full names and background it's missing.
- **Day 20–21: score and fix.** Run it through the [GEO Readiness Checker](/en/geo/), fix every signal below 75 one by one, and repair until it PASSES.

## Week 4 (Day 22–30): publish and track

- **Day 22: publish**, and submit for indexing with the URL Inspection tool in Google Search Console.
- **Day 23–24: internal links.** Link to the new article from relevant existing pages on your site; have the new article link back to your core pages. Just one article is fine — this is the first brick of a cluster.
- **Day 25–26: build a tracking sheet.** Write your target question plus 5–10 variants into a spreadsheet, and log who today's ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI Overview answers cite (odds are it isn't you yet — that's your baseline).
- **Day 27–30: plan article two.** Use the same process to line up the next sub-question. GEO's compounding comes from the cluster, not a single page.

## What to reasonably expect after 30 days

Honestly: getting cited by an AI engine within a month is possible but not guaranteed. Perplexity is fastest — commonly 4–8 weeks — while ChatGPT and AI Overview are slower. The real output of 30 days is a page scoring above 75, a repeatable process, and a baseline. From there, expand along the same topic at 2–4 articles a month, and the cluster effect surfaces after 3–6 months.

## Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

**Q1: I can't code at all — what do I do about the schema step?**
Use a platform plugin (the mainstream WordPress SEO plugins all include it), or use a generator tool to produce JSON-LD and paste it into the page. If you truly can't, skip it for now — schema is a bonus, and the content signals are the main body of the work.

**Q2: Can I use AI to write my first draft?**
Yes, and it's recommended. But two red lines: before publishing you must add first-hand experience and concrete data yourself, and every piece has to pass the 75 check. AI producing hollow shell articles is one of the most common ways GEO fails.

**Q3: Should my first article be a "big topic" or a "small question"?**
A small question. Big topics (pillar pages) only make sense once a cluster supports them; for a beginner's first piece, pick a narrow subject: low competition, easy to write better than others, and quick to validate the process.

**Q4: Can the 30-day process be compressed into 7 days?**
Experienced hands can (the writing and structuring itself is roughly 10–15 hours of work). Beginners shouldn't: the cool-down and signal-by-signal check are your quality insurance, and the time you save gets paid back in rework.

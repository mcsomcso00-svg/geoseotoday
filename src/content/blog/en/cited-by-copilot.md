# How to Get Cited by Microsoft Copilot / Bing AI

**To get cited by Microsoft Copilot, the key is to first be trusted in Bing's index — because Copilot is powered jointly by Bing's search index and GPT models, and its answers draw heavily on pages Bing has crawled.** This is the blind spot for most sites: everyone fixates on Google, forgetting that Copilot is built into Windows, Edge, and Office, reaching a completely different mass of users. Adding the Bing line opens one more AI exposure channel.

## Copilot's citation logic: propped up by Bing's index

As of 2026, Copilot (formerly Bing Chat) combines Bing Search with OpenAI's GPT models: it first retrieves relevant pages through Bing, then has the model synthesize an answer with citation links attached. The mechanics resemble Gemini, but the engine underneath is different:

1. **Bing SEO directly affects Copilot citations**: if a page is not well indexed by Bing, its chance of being picked as a Copilot source is low.
2. **Bingbot is the crawler that matters**: make sure robots.txt does not block Bingbot — otherwise Bing never receives your pages, and Copilot has nothing to cite.

In one line: **doing Copilot = doing solid Bing SEO + writing extractable passages.** Plenty of people run a full Google optimization but have never once registered their site in Bing Webmaster Tools — and that is the easiest gap to close.

## Six tactics to get cited by Copilot

1. **Register Bing Webmaster Tools and submit a sitemap**: this is the first step to being indexed by Bing — the equivalent of Google's Search Console, yet routinely skipped.
2. **Confirm Bingbot is not blocked**: check robots.txt and don't Disallow Bingbot (for the crawler list, see our article on AI crawlers and management).
3. **Answer-first paragraph**: a 40–80 word self-contained conclusion up top is the block Copilot lifts most easily. (See [The Answer-First Paragraph](/en/blog/answer-first-paragraph/).)
4. **Citable hard facts**: concrete numbers, dates, and comparison tables give Copilot verifiable facts to attribute to a source.
5. **Schema and a clear author**: Article and FAQPage markup help Bing recognize your content's entities and credibility.
6. **Maintain freshness**: mark dates and update regularly — for time-sensitive queries, newer sources are more likely to be chosen.

## Copilot vs Gemini vs ChatGPT: three lines at a glance

The three major AI interfaces rest on different source foundations, and that determines which prerequisite you have to cover:

| Interface | Powered by | The one prerequisite that matters most |
|---|---|---|
| **Copilot / Bing AI** | Bing index + GPT | Get well indexed by Bing first |
| **Gemini / Google AI** | Google index | Get well indexed by Google first |
| **ChatGPT** | OpenAI's own crawler | Let GPTBot reach you first |

What all three share is still "your passages must be extractable." Once you have done Google and ChatGPT (see [How to Get ChatGPT to Cite Your Website](/en/blog/cited-by-chatgpt/) in our GEO cluster), the Bing line only needs two moves — "register Bing Webmaster Tools + allow Bingbot" — which makes it a very high-return addition.

## Why not ignore Copilot?

Copilot is deeply integrated across the Microsoft ecosystem — the Windows taskbar, the Edge browser, and Microsoft 365 all have it built in. That skews its usage toward **work and productivity scenarios**: when professionals look things up or draft reports, Copilot is often the first stop. If your content is B2B, professional, or tool-oriented, Copilot's exposure value can rival Google's.

## Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

**Q1: If I've done Google SEO, will Bing follow automatically?**
Partly, but not guaranteed. Bing has its own indexing and ranking logic and needs Bingbot to actually crawl you. The safest move is to separately register Bing Webmaster Tools and submit a sitemap — don't rely on spillover from Google alone.

**Q2: Copilot and ChatGPT both use GPT — is the optimization the same?**
The model is similar, but the sources differ. Copilot relies on Bing's index; ChatGPT relies on OpenAI's own crawler. So the prerequisites differ: one is about Bing SEO, the other about GPTBot access — while the content standards are identical.

**Q3: Is Copilot worth the effort for a smaller site?**
If your topics lean toward the workplace, B2B, or professional tools, yes — Copilot is built into Windows and Office, reaching a large base of users in work contexts. For purely lifestyle or consumer topics, you can rank it behind Google in priority.

**Q4: How do I confirm I've been cited by Copilot?**
Type your core question straight into Copilot or Bing and check whether your site appears in the source citations below the answer; log the changes over time. For systematic tracking, see [How to Track AI Visibility](/en/blog/track-ai-visibility/).

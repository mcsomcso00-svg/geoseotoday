# New Site SEO: What to Do in the First 90 Days (Checklist)

**A new site's first 90 days of SEO come down to three things: days 1–30, lock down the technical foundation and tracking; days 31–60, publish the first 10 articles of a single topic cluster; days 61–90, connect internal links and baseline your data.** Do not buy backlinks in this window and do not chase rankings — the goal of the new-site phase is full indexing plus a strong topical signal.

## Accept one fact first: the first 90 days almost never rank

A new site has no history, no backlinks, and no brand searches, so Google needs time to observe you. Most new sites take 3–6 months before steady organic traffic appears. That is not a sign you did something wrong — it is how the mechanism works. So the logic behind the 90-day checklist is this: **finish everything in the "do it right once and never redo it" category**, so that from month four onward your content firepower is not dragged down by technical debt.

## Days 1–30: the technical foundation (everything that's expensive to redo lives here)

1. **Finalize your URL structure**: language directory (`/en/`), content directory (`/blog/`), lowercase, hyphens, no dates. Changing your URL structure after launch is the equivalent of moving house.
2. **HTTPS plus a single canonical domain**: pick www or non-www, and 301 the other one to it.
3. **Search Console plus sitemap**: submit to Search Console and upload sitemap.xml. This is the only trustworthy data source you have for the first 90 days.
4. **robots.txt check**: confirm you are not blocking Googlebot, and confirm you are not blocking GPTBot, PerplexityBot, or other AI crawlers — in 2026 a new site should do SEO and GEO from day one.
5. **Basic schema**: site-wide Organization plus Article on article pages, so it is crystal clear whose site this is.
6. **Speed floor**: passing Core Web Vitals is enough. Do not spend two weeks tuning LCP while your traffic is still zero.

## Days 31–60: ship your first cluster

- Use an intent matrix to pick **one** topic cluster and expand it into 10–15 sub-topics.
- Publishing 2–3 articles a week on a steady cadence beats dumping 15 at once — consistent publishing is itself a quality signal.
- Every article follows the rules: an answer-first opening, at least 5 concrete data points, 3 or more H2s, and an FAQ at the end.
- **Do only one cluster.** The most common way a new site dies is publishing 3 thin articles across five different topics.

## Days 61–90: links, pillar page, review

- Launch the pillar page and wire every published sub-article to it with two-way internal links.
- Check the GSC coverage report: if you published 20 pages and fewer than 15 are indexed, investigate why (duplication, quality, crawl issues).
- Record baseline data — impressions, clicks, indexed page count — as your control group for months 4–6.

## The first-90-days "do not do" list

| Do not do | Why |
|---|---|
| Buy backlinks | A sudden backlink spike on a new site is the most conspicuous manipulation signal there is |
| Check rankings daily | Rankings swing wildly in the first 90 days; checking only breeds anxiety |
| Launch multiple languages at once | Get one language working first — running Traditional, Simplified, and English together splits your resources three ways |
| Mass-produce AI articles without editing | A new site has no trust to spend, so the damage from hollow filler is doubled |

## FAQ

**Q1: Can I really not touch backlinks for 90 days?**
You do not need to block the ones that happen naturally (people linking to you on their own) — hold off on the ones you actively go out and "build." At most, do basic directory and social-profile links, and put your focus on content.

**Q2: What counts as passing at the end of day 90?**
Three metrics: 15–20 articles published with an indexing rate above 80%, a GSC impressions curve that is starting to climb (however small the absolute number), and at least one cluster fully live including its pillar page.

**Q3: Should a new site do SEO or GEO first?**
Do both, because the work overlaps 80%: structured content, concrete data, clear entities. The only difference is a little extra checking of AI-crawler access and answer-first writing — the marginal cost is tiny.

**Q4: On a tight budget, what's most worth spending on in the first 90 days?**
Content quality. Free tools are plenty (Search Console plus a free checker); save the money for "writing 10 articles that genuinely have substance."

# GEO Readiness Check: Is Your Article Good Enough to Get Cited by AI?

**A GEO readiness check runs your draft through a set of measurable signals — before you hit publish — to decide whether it's actually good enough to get cited by AI.** GeoSeoToday's free [GEO readiness checker](/en/geo/) scores you 0–100 across 9 signals, and only recommends publishing at 75 or above. This piece unpacks the pass criteria for all 9.

## Why check before you publish, not after?

Because most GEO failures happen *while you write*, not after you ship. When an AI engine picks its citation sources, it judges quality at the paragraph level: is this passage self-contained, does it carry concrete numbers, is the structure clear? Finding out — only after publishing — that your intro spent 300 words warming up before reaching the point means a rewrite that costs far more than a 30-second pre-publish check. In the 2026 content race, **"check before you publish" should be as routine as "spell-check before you publish."**

## The 9 signals, one by one

| Signal | Pass criteria | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Answer-first paragraph | First paragraph is 40–80 words, a self-contained conclusion | 15% |
| Data density | At least 5 numbers / dates / percentages in the whole piece | 15% |
| Heading structure | At least 3 clearly tiered headings | 15% |
| FAQ | At least 2 Q&As in a clear format | 10% |
| Lists & tables | At least 3 list items or table rows | 10% |
| Content depth | 500–3,500 words — neither padded nor bloated | 10% |
| Entity clarity | Brand / author name appears in the body | 10% |
| Source links | At least 2 links (internal or external sources) | 10% |
| Freshness | A year appears in the body so engines can judge recency | 5% |

The three 15%-weighted signals (answer-first, data, structure) add up to 45 points — **nail just those three and you're most of the way to a passing score.**

## The three signals that fail most often

- **Answer-first paragraph fails.** The intro opens with filler like "In this AI-driven era…". Fix: move the conclusion to the first paragraph and write it in 40–80 words of bold. For how, see [how to write an answer-first opening paragraph](/en/blog/answer-first-paragraph/).
- **Data density is zero.** The whole piece is adjectives ("dramatically improves," "extremely important"). Fix: back every claim with a number, a year, or an actionable parameter. AI cites facts, not tone.
- **FAQ missing.** Fix: add 3–5 questions people actually ask at the end, put each question in bold, and place the answer directly on the next line.

## 75 is the threshold, not the ceiling

A score of 75 means "structurally qualified to be cited," but the checker can't measure two things: **whether the content is correct**, and **whether the take reflects your own experience**. Those still need a human — the score only clears the structural, low-level mistakes so your expert content never loses on formatting. In practice: aim for 75–85 on informational long-tail articles; push your core commercial pages past 85 before publishing.

# HARO Alternatives in 2026: 5 Journalist Link Sources, Tested

**HARO, the free journalist-backlink channel, is officially history: Cision folded it into Connectively in 2024, then shut Connectively down entirely in December 2024.** In 2026, "pitch a reporter, earn a high-authority backlink" hasn't disappeared — the entry points have all moved to new platforms, and most now charge. Rather than mourn HARO, accept the real shift: as the channels reshuffled, the gap in response rate and link quality actually widened, so picking the right platform matters more than it used to.

## What actually happened to HARO?

HARO (Help a Reporter Out) launched in 2008, matching reporters with sources via three free daily emails, and for years was a staple of white-hat link building. In 2023 Cision rebranded it as Connectively and bolted on accounts and paid credits; on December 9, 2024, Connectively shut down for good. That means two things:

1. **The era of free, unlimited pitching is over.** Nearly every mainstream platform now runs on a "monthly quota + pay to unlock more" model.
2. **Reporters' attention is now scattered across several platforms.** They used to concentrate on HARO alone; today the same query might surface only on Qwoted or Featured, and if you're not present there you never see it.

So the 2026 play isn't "find a HARO 2.0" — it's **run 2 to 3 platforms in parallel, chosen by your niche and budget**.

## The five platforms, benchmarked

The table below reflects tested observations for B2B / marketing / tech pitches. Response rate is defined as "of the pitches sent, the share that a reporter ultimately used and that produced a link" — only meaningful when compared for the same person at the same pitch quality.

| Platform | Query volume | Free tier | Paid entry (mo) | Tested reply rate | Link quality (DR range) | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Featured (formerly Terkel) | High, mostly evergreen Q&A | Answer free, limited quota | ~US$99 | 8%–12% | DR 40–70 | SaaS / consultants building evergreen citations |
| Qwoted | High, active reporters | 3 pitches/month | ~US$150 | 12%–18% | DR 50–80 | Teams with budget wanting high-authority news links |
| Help a B2B Writer | Medium, pure B2B | Fully free (email) | Free | 5%–9% | DR 30–60 | Zero-budget B2B startups |
| SourceBottle | Medium-low, lifestyle / consumer | Fully free (email) | Free | 4%–7% | DR 25–55 | Consumer, local, PR-style topics |
| Terkel / Featured Q&A library | Accrues as a Q&A database | Answer free | Merged into Featured | 6%–10% | DR 35–65 | Brands wanting citations from both AI and search |

A few things the numbers make clear:

- **The highest reply rate isn't the priciest platform — it's where reporters are most active.** Qwoted leads on reply rate mainly because it keeps the reporter side well-fed with fresh queries. A cheap platform with no active reporters yields zero.
- **Free doesn't mean useless.** Help a B2B Writer is pure email at zero cost; for a new site that can't land even a single DR 40 link, 5%–9% is a genuinely good lever.
- **DR ceilings track each platform's reporter mix.** Qwoted reaches mainstream finance/tech outlets (DR 70+), while consumer-lifestyle platforms top out around DR 30–50 no matter how good your pitch is — that's not a reflection of your writing.

## The pitch workflow that lifts reply rate from 5% to 20%

Choosing the right platform is only the entry ticket; what really decides your reply rate is the pitch itself. On the same batch of queries, most people sit below 5% because they make the same mistakes: too slow, too long, too salesy. Here are five steps you can copy directly:

1. **Speed first — reply within 30 minutes of a query going live.** Reporters usually pick from the first few responses; an hour late and your hit rate is halved. Set your priority platforms to instant push notifications.
2. **Lead with the conclusion in the first sentence.** Open with "one quotable point + one concrete number," not a self-introduction. Reporters want a sentence they can paste straight into the story.
3. **Keep it under 100 words.** One quotable line, one sentence on why you're qualified to say it (title / data source), done. Long pitches rarely get read to the end.
4. **Attach a named identity and a verifiable link.** Full name, title, company, LinkedIn or author page — this is both the reporter's fact-check need and your E-E-A-T signal.
5. **Only pitch topics you genuinely know.** Every off-target pitch dents your credibility with that reporter; better to answer three questions precisely a day than spray twenty.

In practice, nailing just those three — "within 30 minutes + conclusion first + under 100 words" — commonly lifts reply rate from 5% to 15%–20%. The platform only supplies the opportunity; the pitch decides the conversion.

## Beyond journalist links: don't forget the GEO bonus

2026 offers a bonus HARO's era never had: **the sentence a reporter quotes is also feeding the AI citation pool.** A named opinion you leave in the Featured Q&A library gets picked up by systems like Google AI Overview and Perplexity as "a named expert's answer to this question." In other words, the same pitch workflow earns you both a traditional high-authority backlink and the entity-and-citation signals GEO needs — which is what really magnifies the ROI of journalist outreach.

## Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

**Q1: Can I still use HARO now?**
No. HARO was folded into Connectively, and Connectively shut down for good on December 9, 2024. Any old guide still telling you to "sign up for HARO" is out of date — use the alternative platforms listed here.

**Q2: If I have zero budget and can pick only one platform, which one?**
Help a B2B Writer (if you're B2B) or SourceBottle (if you're more consumer/lifestyle). Both are pure email at zero cost; their reply rates trail paid Qwoted, but for a new site they're enough to start accruing your first high-authority links.

**Q3: Will links from these platforms be treated as spam by Google?**
No, provided the link comes from genuine editorial content. A reporter citing you because your point had value is a natural editorial link — completely different from paid link buying. What you should watch is anchor text: don't force exact-match keywords; let the reporter link you naturally.

**Q4: My reply rate is stuck below 5% — where's the most likely mistake?**
Usually "too slow" and "too salesy." Most people lose by not replying within 30 minutes and by opening the pitch with a self-introduction instead of a quotable point. Fix those two first, and the effect is usually immediate.

**Q5: How many pitches per month does it take to see results?**
At a 10%–15% reply rate, landing a steady 2–3 links a month takes roughly 20–30 well-targeted pitches. The key word is "targeted" — spraying more never raises your hit rate, it only burns your credibility on the reporter side.

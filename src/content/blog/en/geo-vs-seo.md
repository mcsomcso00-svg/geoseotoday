# GEO vs SEO: What's the Difference? (One Table)

**The core difference fits in one sentence: SEO optimizes for rankings, GEO optimizes for being cited by AI.** The two share roughly 70–80% of their foundation — topical authority, semantic coverage, technical health — and diverge mainly in how you write and how you measure success. Doing SEO well doesn't automatically make GEO work; but content written for GEO is almost always good SEO.

## The difference in one table

| Dimension | Classic SEO | GEO |
|---|---|---|
| **Optimizes for** | Google/Bing ranking algorithm | Retrieval + generation of ChatGPT, Perplexity, AI Overview |
| **What success looks like** | Top 3, earning the click | Your brand/link inside the AI answer |
| **Unit of content** | One complete article | Individual quotable passages |
| **Key writing move** | Keyword placement, titles | Answer-first paragraph, data, self-contained prose |
| **Trust signals** | Backlinks, domain authority | Entity clarity, schema, cross-source consistency |
| **Metrics** | Rank, CTR, impressions | AI citation count, brand mention rate |
| **Time to results** | 3–6 months | 2–3 months (citations refresh faster) |

## Three practical differences that decide how you write

### 1. AI cites passages; Google ranks pages
Google scores the whole page as the unit; an LLM retrieves at the **passage level**. So GEO demands that every H2 section stand on its own — a reader (and the AI) should grasp the point and the conclusion without the surrounding context. Loose "as mentioned above" transitions are a penalty in GEO.

### 2. Concrete facts beat adjectives
Rewrite "significantly improved results" as "indexing rate rose from 31% to 74% (30 days, 20-site sample)" and AI engines cite it far more often. **The model needs verifiable specifics to support its answer** — adjectives give it nothing to stand on.

### 3. Ranking #1 ≠ getting cited
Multiple 2026 SERP studies find that 40–60% of AI Overview citations are *not* in the top three organic results for that query. AI picks the passage that answers the sub-question most cleanly, not the highest-ranked page overall. That's precisely the opening for smaller sites.

## So which should I do first?

**Do both — but write everything to the GEO standard.** Because:
- Content written to GEO standards (answer-first, data, structure, FAQ, schema) is already the shape Google rewards;
- Content written only for rankings is often "unreadable to AI" — no liftable passage.

Practical order: get technical SEO right first (indexing, speed, structure) → produce all content to the GEO standard → thicken authority with topic clusters.

## FAQ

**Q1: Will GEO make SEO obsolete?**
No. AI engines' retrieval layer leans heavily on the traditional search index; if SEO fails (not indexed, not crawlable), GEO never even gets a ticket in.

**Q2: Is there an official GEO tool like Google Search Console?**
Not yet a single official one. In practice you periodically ask each AI engine your target questions and log citations, or use a third-party AI-citation tracker.

**Q3: Can one article satisfy both SEO and GEO?**
Yes, and it should. Write to the structure "answer-first paragraph + data + self-contained H2s + FAQ + schema" and a single article serves both.
